# Summary of changes
## Summary

This project contains IMO 1970 Problem 6: proving that among 100 points in general position in the plane, at most 70% of the triangles they form can be acute.

### What was accomplished

I proved the **key algebraic lemma** (`four_point_algebraic` in `Submission.lean`, lines 35-62) which establishes that for any 4 points in the plane (after normalizing coordinates), the 12 polynomial conditions for all 4 triangles to be simultaneously acute are mutually inconsistent. This is the core mathematical insight behind the 70% bound.

The proof works by:
1. Parametrizing the fourth point D = (d₁, d₂) as d₁ = α + β·c₁, d₂ = β·c₂ (decomposing D in the basis given by B and C from the origin A)
2. Performing case analysis on the signs of α and β  
3. Using `nlinarith` with carefully chosen polynomial products to derive contradictions in each case

### What remains

The main theorem `imo1970_p6` (line 72) still has a `sorry`. Bridging the gap between the algebraic lemma and the full theorem requires:

1. **Geometric 4-point lemma**: Showing the algebraic result applies to arbitrary 4 points via coordinate normalization (translation + rotation preserve inner product signs)
2. **5-point counting lemma**: Among any 5 points in general position, at most 7 of the 10 triangles are acute (follows from the 4-point lemma by double counting over C(5,4)=5 quadruples)
3. **Double counting argument**: Using the 5-point lemma over all C(100,5) quintuples to bound the ratio
4. **Nat.card infrastructure**: Connecting the combinatorial counting to the `Nat.card` formulation used in the problem statement

Each of these steps requires substantial formalization effort. The problem is a competition-level geometry result (IMO Problem 6) and full formalization would typically require weeks of expert effort.
