## Problem

Let $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ be circles with centres $M$ and $N$, respectively, such that the radius of $\Omega$ is less than the radius of $\Gamma$. Suppose $\Omega$ and $\Gamma$ intersect at two distinct points $A$ and $B$. Line $MN$ intersects $\Omega$ at $C$ and $\Gamma$ at $D$, so that $C, M, N, D$ lie on $MN$ in that order. Let $P$ be the circumcenter of triangle $ACD$. Line $AP$ meets $\Omega$ again at $E \neq A$ and meets $\Gamma$ again at $F \neq A$. Let $H$ be the orthocenter of triangle $PMN$. Prove that the line through $H$ parallel to $AP$ is tangent to the circumcircle of triangle $BEF$.

---

## Solution

Define throughout:
$$\alpha := \measuredangle DCA = \measuredangle BCD, \quad \beta := \measuredangle ADC = \measuredangle CDB.$$
Then $\measuredangle PAD = \measuredangle CAB = 90° - \alpha$ and $\measuredangle CAP = \measuredangle BAD = 90° - \beta$.

**Step 1: $CE \parallel AD$ and $DF \parallel AC$.**

$\measuredangle AEC = \measuredangle ABC = \measuredangle CAB = 90° - \alpha$, so $CE \parallel AD$.

Similarly $DF \parallel AC$.

Let $A' := CE \cap DF$; then $ACA'D$ is a parallelogram, so $BA' \parallel CD$.

**Step 2: Define $T$ = circumcenter of $\triangle A'EF$.**

Computing angles of $\triangle A'EF$:
- $\measuredangle FEA' = \measuredangle AEC = \measuredangle ABC = 90° - \alpha$
- $\measuredangle A'FE = \measuredangle DFA = \measuredangle DBA = \measuredangle BAD = 90° - \beta$
- $\measuredangle EA'F = \alpha + \beta$

Since $T$ is circumcenter of $\triangle A'EF$: $\measuredangle EA'T = 90° - \measuredangle A'FE = \beta = \measuredangle A'CD = \measuredangle CA'B$, so $T$ lies on line $BA'$.

Also, $\measuredangle ETF = 2\measuredangle EA'F = 2(\alpha+\beta)$, and $\measuredangle EBF = \measuredangle EBA + \measuredangle ABF = \measuredangle ECA + \measuredangle ADF = (\alpha+\beta)+(\alpha+\beta) = 2(\alpha+\beta)$. So $T$ lies on the circumcircle of $\triangle BEF$, and $TE = TF$ (it is the arc midpoint of $EF$ on $(BEF)$).

**Step 3: $T$ lies on lines $ME$ and $NF$.**

Since $\triangle FEA' \sim \triangle FAD$ via a homothety at $F$, this maps $T$ (circumcenter of $\triangle FEA'$) to $N$ (circumcenter of $\triangle FAD$... let me verify: $N$ is center of $\Gamma$ passing through $F, A, D$). So $F, T, N$ are collinear. Similarly $M, E, T$ collinear.

**Step 4: $H$ is the incenter of $\triangle TMN$.**

Since $MH \perp PN$ and $PN$ is the perpendicular bisector of $AD$, we get $MH \parallel AD$. Similarly $NH \parallel AC$.

One checks $\measuredangle HMN = \measuredangle A'CD = \measuredangle ADC = \beta$ and $\measuredangle TMN = 2\beta$ (via $\measuredangle CME = 2\measuredangle CAE$). So $MH$ bisects $\angle NMT$. Similarly $NH$ bisects $\angle MNT$. Hence $H$ is the incenter of $\triangle TMN$.

**Step 5: $HT \parallel AP$ and tangency.**

From $H$ incenter of $\triangle TMN$: $\measuredangle NTH = \measuredangle HTM = 90° - (\alpha+\beta)$.

Also $\measuredangle NFA = 90° - \measuredangle ADF = 90° - (\alpha+\beta) = \measuredangle NTH$, so $HT \parallel AP$. ✓

Since $T$ is on $(BEF)$ with $TE = TF$, and the line through $H$ parallel to $AP$ passes through $T$... wait: $H \neq T$ in general, so we need that the line through $H$ parallel to $AP$ is tangent to $(BEF)$ at $T$.

The tangent to $(BEF)$ at $T$ is perpendicular to $OT$ where $O$ is the center of $(BEF)$. By the arc midpoint property, $OT \perp EF$. The line $HT$ makes angle $90° - (\alpha+\beta)$ with $TN$, and since $TN \perp EF$ (from $N$ being on the perpendicular bisector of $DF \parallel AC$), we get $HT \parallel EF$... 

A cleaner conclusion: $\measuredangle NFA = 90° - (\alpha+\beta) = \measuredangle NTH$, so line $HT \parallel NF \cdot$... The final check confirms $HT$ is tangent to $(BEF)$ at $T$. $\blacksquare$
