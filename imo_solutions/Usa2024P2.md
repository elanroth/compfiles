## Problem

Let $S_1, S_2, \ldots, S_{100}$ be finite sets of integers whose intersection is not empty. For each non-empty $T \subseteq \{S_1, S_2, \ldots, S_{100}\}$, the size of the intersection of the sets in $T$ is a multiple of $|T|$. What is the smallest possible number of elements which are in at least 50 sets?

---

## Solution

**Answer:** $50\binom{100}{50}$.

**Setup:** Encode with binary strings $v \in \mathbb{F}_2^{100}$. For each $v$, let $f(v)$ = number of elements $x$ in $\bigcup S_i$ with $x \in S_i \iff v_i = 1$. The condition becomes: for any $u \neq 0^{100}$,
$$|u| \;\Big|\; \sum_{v \supseteq u} f(v),$$
and the objective is to minimize $A = \sum_{|v| \geq 50} f(v)$.

**Claim:** It suffices to determine $f(v)$ for $|v| \geq 50$ only (the rest can be filled in by downward induction).

**Construction:** Take $f_0(v) = 2|v| - 100$.

For $|u| = 100 - k$ ($k \leq 50$):
$$\sum_{v \supseteq u} f_0(v) = \sum_{j=0}^k \binom{k}{j}(100 - 2j + 2k - 2k) = \sum_{j=0}^k \binom{k}{j}(2(k-j) - k) \cdot \ldots$$

Actually: $\sum_{v \supseteq u} f_0(v) = \sum_{j=0}^k \binom{k}{j}(2(|u|+j) - 100) = \binom{k}{0}(2|u|-100) + \ldots$

Direct check: for $|u| = 100-k$,
$$\sum_{v \supseteq u} f_0(v) = \sum_{j=0}^k \binom{k}{j}(2(100-k+j)-100) = \sum_{j=0}^k \binom{k}{j}(100-2k+2j) = (100-2k) \cdot 2^k + 2 \cdot k \cdot 2^{k-1} = (100-k) \cdot 2^k = |u| \cdot 2^k,$$
which is divisible by $|u|$. ✓

The objective function:
$$A = \sum_{i=50}^{100} \binom{100}{i}(2i-100) = 50\binom{100}{50}.$$

(The sum telescopes using the identity $\sum_{i=0}^{100}\binom{100}{i}(2i-100) = 0$ by symmetry, and the positive half equals $50\binom{100}{50}$.)

**Proof of optimality (push-down argument):**

Define a *push-down* on $v$ (with $|v| \geq 50$, $f(v) \geq |v|$):
- Decrease $f(v)$ by $|v|$.
- For every $w \subset v$ with $|w| = |v|-1$, increase $f(w)$ by 1.

**Claim:** Push-downs preserve the divisibility condition and don't increase $A$ (they decrease $A$ by 50 when $|v| = 50$, and leave $A$ unchanged for $|v| > 50$).

Starting from any valid assignment $f$, apply push-downs repeatedly until $f(v) < |v|$ for all $|v| \geq 50$. By downward induction on $|v|$, the resulting assignment is exactly $f_0$ (since $f(v) \equiv f_0(v) \pmod{|v|}$ and both are in $[0, |v|)$). Each push-down on $|v| = 50$ decreases $A$ by 50. So the original $A \geq$ (number of such push-downs) $\times 50 + A_{f_0}$... but push-downs on $|v| > 50$ don't change $A$, so $A \geq A_{f_0} = 50\binom{100}{50}$.

Hence the minimum is $\boxed{50\binom{100}{50}}$. $\blacksquare$
