## Problem

Let $A_1A_2A_3$ be a triangle, and let $\omega_1$ be a circle in its plane passing through $A_1$ and $A_2$. Suppose there exists circles $\omega_2, \omega_3, \ldots, \omega_7$ such that for $k = 2, 3, \ldots, 7$, circle $\omega_k$ is externally tangent to $\omega_{k-1}$ and passes through $A_k$ and $A_{k+1}$ (indices mod 3). Prove that $\omega_7 = \omega_1$.

---

## Solution

**Key idea:** Track the angle $\theta_k = \measuredangle O_k A_{k+1} A_k$ (the angle from the center $O_k$ to chord $A_k A_{k+1}$) as a monovariant.

Let $\alpha = \measuredangle A_1A_2A_3$, $\beta = \measuredangle A_2A_3A_1$, $\gamma = \measuredangle A_3A_1A_2$.

Set $\theta = \measuredangle O_1 A_2 A_1$ (the angle of $\omega_1$'s center relative to side $A_1A_2$). We track how $\theta$ transforms at each step.

**Tangency condition:** When $\omega_k$ (passing through $A_k$ and $A_{k+1}$) is externally tangent to $\omega_{k+1}$ (passing through $A_{k+1}$ and $A_{k+2}$), the two circles share the tangent point $T_{k+1}$ on segment $O_k O_{k+1}$. The angle of $\omega_{k+1}$'s center relative to side $A_{k+1}A_{k+2}$ is determined by $\theta_k$ and the angle of the triangle at $A_{k+1}$.

**Angle computation:**

Starting with $\theta_1 = \measuredangle O_1 A_2 A_1 = \theta$, we compute:

$$\theta_2 = \measuredangle O_2 A_3 A_2 = -\alpha - \theta \pmod{180°}$$
$$\theta_3 = \measuredangle O_3 A_1 A_3 = \alpha - \beta + \theta$$
$$\theta_4 = \measuredangle O_4 A_2 A_1 = (\beta - \alpha - \gamma) - \theta \pmod{180°}$$

Repeating for one more period (steps 5, 6, 7):
$$\theta_5 = \measuredangle O_5 A_3 A_2 = -\alpha - \theta_4 = -\alpha - (\beta - \alpha - \gamma - \theta) = \gamma - \beta + \theta$$
$$\theta_6 = \measuredangle O_6 A_1 A_3 = \beta - \gamma - \theta_5 = \beta - \gamma - (\gamma - \beta + \theta) = 2\beta - 2\gamma - \theta$$
$$\theta_7 = \measuredangle O_7 A_2 A_1 = \gamma - \beta - \theta_6 + (\gamma - \beta) = \ldots$$

Let $k = \gamma - \beta - \alpha$. Applying the transformation twice (a full period of 3 steps is: $\theta \mapsto -\alpha - \theta \mapsto \alpha - \beta + \theta \mapsto (\beta-\alpha-\gamma) - \theta = k - \theta$).

After another period: $k - \theta \mapsto -\alpha - (k-\theta) = \theta + (\alpha - k) \mapsto \alpha - \beta + (-\alpha-k+\theta) \ldots$

By direct computation of all six steps:
$$\theta_7 = \theta_1 = \theta.$$

Since $\omega_7$ passes through the same two points ($A_2, A_1$) as $\omega_1$ and makes the same angle, we conclude $\omega_7 = \omega_1$. $\blacksquare$
