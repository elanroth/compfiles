## Problem

Let $n$ be a positive integer. A *Nordic square* is an $n \times n$ board containing all the integers from 1 to $n^2$ so that each cell contains exactly one number. An *uphill path* is a sequence of one or more cells such that: (a) the first cell is a *valley* (its number is less than all orthogonal neighbors), (b) each subsequent cell is orthogonally adjacent to the previous, and (c) the numbers are in increasing order.

Find, as a function of $n$, the smallest possible total number of uphill paths in a Nordic square.

---

## Solution

**Answer:** $2n^2 - 2n + 1$.

**Lower bound:** We show there are always at least $2n^2 - 2n + 1$ uphill paths.

- *Paths of length $\geq 2$:* For any pair of orthogonally adjacent cells with $a > b$ (i.e., the larger is adjacent to the smaller), extend downward to a valley to get an uphill path of length $\geq 2$ passing through the edge $ab$ (from smaller to larger). There are $2n(n-1)$ such directed edges (horizontal and vertical), giving $2n(n-1) = 2n^2 - 2n$ uphill paths of length $\geq 2$.
- *Paths of length 1:* There is always at least one valley (the cell containing 1 is a valley), giving at least 1 path of length 1.

Total: at least $(2n^2 - 2n) + 1 = 2n^2 - 2n + 1$.

**Construction achieving equality:** Build a spanning tree $T$ on the grid such that no two cells *not* in $T$ are adjacent. Then label the cells so that:
- Cell 1 is the only valley.
- All tree edges go "uphill" (smaller number to larger).
- Non-tree edges go from a high number to a lower one (no pair of adjacent high-numbered cells outside $T$ creates new paths).

For $n$ divisible by 3, the following construction works: place a tree $T$ (shown as a "spine" zigzag pattern) covering about $2n^2/3$ cells. Label cells in $T$ with $1, 2, \ldots, |T|$ in tree order, then fill the remaining cells with $|T|+1, \ldots, n^2$ arbitrarily.

Since 1 is the unique valley, every uphill path starts from 1. Each path of length $\geq 2$ corresponds to a directed edge (from smaller to larger), and in this construction each such edge is counted exactly once. The total count is:
$$1 + 2n(n-1) = 2n^2 - 2n + 1.$$

For general $n$, the construction is modified (delete last $n \bmod 3$ rows and adjust) to still achieve this count.

**Conclusion:** The minimum number of uphill paths is $\boxed{2n^2 - 2n + 1}$. $\blacksquare$
