## Problem

We call a positive integer *alternating* if every two consecutive digits in its decimal representation are of different parity. Find all positive integers $n$ which have an alternating multiple.

---

## Solution

**Answer:** All positive integers $n$ not divisible by 20 have an alternating multiple.

**Step 1: $20 \mid n$ fails.** If $20 \mid n$, then any multiple of $n$ ends in $\ldots 0$, with the tens digit even (from $20k$), so consecutive digits $\ldots \text{even} \cdot 0$ — both even. No alternating multiple exists.

**Step 2: All $n$ with $\gcd(n, 10) = 1$ work (Head construction).**

For $\gcd(n, 10) = 1$: The number $\underbrace{10\,10\,\cdots\,10}_{m \text{ copies}}$ is divisible by $n$ for $m = \varphi(99n)$ (standard argument). By modifying individual digits between the 1's and 0's using multiples of $10^{\varphi(n)}$ (which are $\equiv 1 \pmod n$), we can make the number congruent to any desired residue modulo $n$, while keeping the pattern alternating.

**Step 3: Tail constructions for powers of 2 and 5.**

**Claim (Tail construction):** For every even integer $w \geq 2$:
- There exists an even alternating multiple $g(w)$ of $2^{w+1}$ with exactly $w$ digits.
- There exists an even alternating multiple $h(w)$ of $5^w$ with exactly $w$ digits.

*Proof by induction.* Base: $g(2) = 32$, $h(2) = 50$. For the inductive step, prepend a two-digit alternating string from the set $\{10, 12, 14, \ldots, 98\}$ to $g(w)$; this set covers all residues modulo $2^{w+3}$ (they are all divisible by $2^{w+1}$). Similarly for $h$.

**Step 4: General case.**

Any $n$ not divisible by 20 falls into one of:
- $n = 2^k m$ with $\gcd(m, 10) = 1$, $k$ even, $k \geq 2$: concatenate $10^k \cdot f(-g(k)/10^k \bmod m) + g(k)$.
- $n = 5^k m$ with $\gcd(m, 10) = 1$, $k$ even, $k \geq 2$: concatenate similarly using $h(k)$.
- $n = 50m$ with $\gcd(m, 10) = 1$: use $100 \cdot f(-1/2 \bmod m) + 50$.
- All other cases reduce via the head construction.

Since every non-multiple of 20 falls into one of these cases, every such $n$ has an alternating multiple.

**Conclusion:** The positive integers $n$ having an alternating multiple are precisely those **not divisible by 20**. $\blacksquare$
