## Problem

Let $m \geq 2$ be an integer, $A$ a finite set of integers (not necessarily positive) and $B_1, B_2, \ldots, B_m$ subsets of $A$. Suppose that, for every $k = 1, 2, \ldots, m$, the sum of the elements of $B_k$ is $m^k$. Prove that $A$ contains at least $\frac{m}{2}$ elements.

---

## Solution

**Key idea:** Write multiples of $m$ in base $m$ and use them to extract information.

For $0 \leq X < m^{m+1}$ a multiple of $m$, write $X = \sum_{i=1}^{m} c_i m^i$ with $c_i \in \{0, 1, \ldots, m-1\}$ (base-$m$ representation). Then:
$$X = \sum_{i=1}^{m} c_i m^i = \sum_{i=1}^{m} c_i \left(\sum_{b \in B_i} b\right) = \sum_{a \in A} \left(\sum_{i : a \in B_i} c_i\right) a = \sum_{a \in A} f_a(X) \cdot a,$$
where $f_a(X) := \sum_{i: a \in B_i} c_i$.

Since $c_i \in \{0, \ldots, m-1\}$, we have $0 \leq f_a(X) \leq m(m-1)$ for any $a$ and $X$.

**Counting:** The map $X \mapsto (f_a(X))_{a \in A}$ takes values in $\{0, 1, \ldots, m(m-1)\}^{|A|}$, which has $(m(m-1)+1)^{|A|}$ elements.

The inputs $X$ range over multiples of $m$ in $[0, m^{m+1})$, of which there are $m^m$ values.

Since the map is well-defined (each $X$ determines the $c_i$'s uniquely, which determine the $f_a$'s), we need at least as many outputs as inputs:
$$(m(m-1)+1)^{|A|} \geq m^m.$$

Taking logarithms:
$$|A| \cdot \log(m(m-1)+1) \geq m \log m.$$

Since $m(m-1)+1 \leq m^2$, we have $\log(m(m-1)+1) \leq 2\log m$, giving:
$$|A| \geq \frac{m \log m}{2 \log m} = \frac{m}{2}.$$

Therefore $|A| \geq \lceil m/2 \rceil \geq m/2$. $\blacksquare$

*Remark (motivation): In linear algebra terms, we have $m$ vectors $\mathbf{v}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{v}_m \in \{0,1\}^n$ (indicator vectors of $B_k$ in $A$) and a vector $\mathbf{a}$ of elements such that $\mathbf{v}_k \cdot \mathbf{a} = m^k$. If $n$ is small, Siegel's lemma gives linear dependencies with bounded coefficients, but any linear combination $\sum \lambda_k m^k = 0$ with $|\lambda_k| < m$ is impossible — so $n$ must be large enough.*
