## Problem

There is an integer $n > 1$. There are $n^2$ stations on a slope of a mountain, all at different altitudes. Each of two cable car companies, $A$ and $B$, operates $k$ cable cars; each cable car provides a transfer from one of the stations to a higher one (with no intermediate stops). The $k$ cable cars of $A$ have $k$ different starting points and $k$ different finishing points, and a cable car which starts higher also finishes higher. The same conditions hold for $B$. We say that two stations are *linked* by a company if one can start from the lower station and reach the higher one by using one or more cars of that company. Determine the smallest positive integer $k$ for which one can guarantee that there are two stations linked by both companies.

---

## Solution

**Answer:** $k = n^2 - n + 1$.

**Step 1: Construction showing $k = n^2 - n$ is insufficient.**

The construction for $n = 4$ (generalized below): arrange the $n^2$ stations in an $n \times n$ grid with rows $R_1, \ldots, R_n$ (bottom to top) and columns $C_1, \ldots, C_n$. Company $A$ operates cars connecting station $(i, j)$ to station $(i+1, j)$ for all valid $i, j$ (staying within the same column — $n(n-1)$ cars, which equals $n^2 - n$). Company $B$ operates cars connecting $(i, j)$ to $(i, j+1)$ (within the same row).

In this setup, $A$ links only stations in the same column, and $B$ links only stations in the same row. No two stations are linked by both companies. This uses $k = n^2 - n = n(n-1)$.

**Step 2: $k = n^2 - n + 1$ guarantees a common linked pair.**

View $A$ and $B$ as directed graphs on $n^2$ vertices (stations), where each is a graph whose connected components are directed paths (chains). With $k = n^2 - n + 1$ edges, each graph has at most $n^2 - k = n - 1$ connected components (since a path on $m$ vertices has $m - 1$ edges, and a forest on $n^2$ vertices with $k$ edges has $n^2 - k$ components).

So $A$ has at most $n - 1$ connected components. By the pigeonhole principle, some component of $A$ has at least $\frac{n^2}{n-1} > n + 1$ vertices, hence at least $n + 1$ stations.

Among any $n + 1$ stations, two must lie in the same connected component of $B$ (since $B$ has at most $n - 1$ components, and $n + 1 > n - 1$). Those two stations are linked by both $A$ and $B$. $\blacksquare$

*Remark:* The condition "a cable car starting higher also finishes higher" is actually superfluous for the bound; it only affects which constructions are valid.
