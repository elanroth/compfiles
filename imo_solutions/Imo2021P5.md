## Problem

Two squirrels, Bushy and Jumpy, have collected 2021 walnuts for the winter. Jumpy numbers the walnuts from 1 through 2021, and digs 2021 little holes in a circular pattern in the ground around their favourite tree. The next morning Jumpy notices that Bushy had placed one walnut into each hole, but had paid no attention to the numbering. Unhappy, Jumpy decides to reorder the walnuts by performing a sequence of 2021 moves. In the $k$th move, Jumpy swaps the positions of the two walnuts adjacent to walnut $k$.

Prove that there exists a value of $k$ such that, on the $k$th move, Jumpy swaps some walnuts $a$ and $b$ such that $a < k < b$.

---

## Solution

Assume for contradiction no such $k$ exists. We use a "threshold trick" / parity argument.

**Coloring process:** This process takes exactly 2021 steps. Right after the $k$th move, color walnut $k$ red. At the $k$th step there are $k$ red walnuts.

**Key observation:** By our assumption, at each step the walnut that becomes red is between two non-red or two red walnuts (never between one red and one black).

**Parity obstruction:**

**Claim 1:** At each step, the walnut becoming red is between two walnuts of the same color.
*Proof:* By assumption.

**Claim 2:** After the first step, there is always a consecutive block of black (non-red) walnuts of positive **even** length.
*Proof:* After step 1, there are 2020 black walnuts in a circular arc — a single block of length 2020, which is even.

**Inductive maintenance:** We show that an even-length block of black walnuts persists.
- A length-2 block of black walnuts can never be destroyed: if a red walnut is placed inside it, it would need to be adjacent to the boundary (between a red and a black), contradicting Claim 1.
- For even lengths $\geq 4$: if a red walnut is placed inside such a block, the block splits into two pieces. One piece has odd length, one has even length (since even minus 1 split into two parts summing to an odd number — so one is odd, one is even). Thus an even-length block survives.

Since 2021 is odd, when all 2021 steps are complete all holes would be red — but the claim implies an even-length block of black walnuts persists forever, a contradiction (we'd need at least 2 black walnuts remaining at the end, but we run 2021 moves turning all walnuts red).

This contradiction proves the assumption was false: there must exist some $k$ with $a < k < b$. $\blacksquare$

*Remark: The statement is true with 2021 replaced by any odd number, and false for any even number.*
