## Problem

Let $D$ be an interior point of the acute triangle $ABC$ with $AB > AC$ so that $\angle DAB = \angle CAD$. The point $E$ on the segment $AC$ satisfies $\angle ADE = \angle BCD$, the point $F$ on the segment $AB$ satisfies $\angle FDA = \angle DBC$, and the point $X$ on the line $AC$ satisfies $CX = BX$. Let $O_1$ and $O_2$ be the circumcenters of triangles $ADC$ and $EXD$, respectively. Prove that the lines $BC$, $EF$, and $O_1O_2$ are concurrent.

---

## Solution

*(Solution contributed by Abdullahil Kafi.)*

**Step 1: $BCEF$ is cyclic.**

Let $D'$ be the isogonal conjugate of $D$ with respect to triangle $ABC$. The angle conditions imply quadrilaterals $CEDD'$ and $BFDD'$ are both cyclic. By power of a point:
$$AE \cdot AC = AD \cdot AD' = AF \cdot AB,$$
so $BCEF$ is cyclic.

**Step 2: Line $ZD$ is tangent to $(BCD)$ and $(DEF)$, where $Z = EF \cap BC$.**

Let $\angle CAD = \angle BAD = \alpha$, $\angle BCD = \beta$, $\angle DBC = \gamma$, $\angle ACD = \phi$, $\angle ABD = \epsilon$. From $\triangle ABC$: $2\alpha + \beta + \gamma + \phi + \epsilon = 180Â°$.

Let $\ell$ be a tangent line to $(BCD)$. Computing angles:
$$\angle AFE = \beta + \phi, \quad \angle BFD = \alpha + \gamma, \quad \angle DFE = \alpha + \phi, \quad \angle CDL = \alpha + \phi,$$
where $L = AD \cap BC$. Then $\angle CDJ = 180Â° - \gamma - \beta - (\alpha+\phi) = \alpha + \epsilon$, so $\angle DFE = \angle EDK = \alpha + \epsilon$, meaning $\ell$ is also tangent to $(DEF)$. By the radical center theorem, $\ell$ passes through $Z$.

**Step 3: Concurrency via the Miquel point.**

Let $M$ be the Miquel point of the cyclic quadrilateral $BCEF$. From the Miquel configuration, $A$, $M$, $Z$ are collinear and $(AFEM)$, $(ZCEM)$ are cyclic.

**Step 4: $B, X, M, E$ are cyclic.**

$\angle EMB = 180Â° - \angle AMB - \angle EMZ = 180Â° - 2\angle ACB = \angle EXB$.

**Step 5: $B, D, M, N$ are cyclic** (where $N$ is the second intersection of $(ACD)$ and $(DEX)$).

Let $R = AC \cap BM$. By power of a point:
$$\text{Pow}(R,(ACD)) = RC \cdot RA = RM \cdot RB = RE \cdot RX = \text{Pow}(R,(DEX)),$$
so $R$ lies on the radical axis of $(ACD)$ and $(DEX)$, giving $N$, $R$, $D$ collinear. Then $RN \cdot RD = RA \cdot RC = RM \cdot RB$, so $BDMN$ is cyclic.

**Step 6: Conclusion.**

The circles $(ACD)$, $(BDMN)$, $(DEX)$ are coaxial (their centers are collinear). Now take $\omega$ centered at $Z$ with radius $ZD$. By power of a point: $ZC \cdot ZB = ZD^2 = ZE \cdot ZF = ZM \cdot ZA$, so inversion in $\omega$ swaps $(ACD)$ and $(BDMN)$. Hence the centers of $(ACD)$ and $(BDMN)$ are collinear with $Z$ â€” which means $O_1$, $O_2$, and $Z$ are collinear, as desired. $\blacksquare$
